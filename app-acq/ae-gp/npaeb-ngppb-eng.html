INITIATIVES:
Proposed Standard Environmental Considerations for all Contracts
GoC Commitment to Green Procurement
Advance the protection of the environment and support sustainable development by integrating environmental performance considerations into the procurement decision-making process.        
 
Reports and billing
Invoices and correspondence to be sent and processed electronically.        
Use of electronic signatures, where client capacity to accept them exists, to reduce paper consumption.        
Draft reports are sent electronically.        
Limitations on number of hard copies of documents provided.        
Paper is certified as originating from a sustainably managed forest and/or has a minimum of 30% recycled content.        
 
Reduction/Reuse
Commodity group has taken action to encourage reduction in consumption.        
Commodity group has taken action to encourage sharing of surplus assets.        
Commodity group has identified alternatives to procurement.        
*…edit and/or add further initiatives, as appropriate…

TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS AND CRITERIA
1. Manufacturer's commitment to the environment
Manufacturer's environmental policy statement.        
Manufacturer has history on environmental initiatives.        
Manufacturer has conducted waste audit.        
Manufacturer has plans for environmental improvements.        
ISO 14000 accreditation.        
*…edit and/or add further manufacturer commitments, as appropriate…        
 
2. Resource input
Use of non-organic solvent-based adhesives.        
Metal components finished using low VOC emitting technology.        
Metal parts contain recycled content.        
Plastic parts contain recycled content.        
Major rigid plastic parts stamped with composition code for recycling.        
*…edit and/or add further resource-related actions, as appropriate…        
 
3. Product design
Major component designed to be disassembled.        
Wear susceptible parts are designed to be replaceable by the user.        
*…edit and/or add further product design elements, as appropriate…        
 
4. Manufacturing
Waste material from the manufacturing process is minimized.        
Waste material from the manufacturing process is recycled.        
*…edit and/or add further manufacturing elements, as appropriate…        
 
5. Packaging and Distribution
Packaging is designed to minimize waste.        
Recyled materials are used to produce the packaging.        
Supplier accepts the packaging for reuse, recycling, or recovery.        
*…edit and/or add further packaging initiatives, as appropriate…        
 
6. Use and Maintenance
Product is accompanied by clear maintenance and repair instructions.        
Inventory numbers for replacement parts are available.        
Warranty for 10 to 20 years, according to product.        
*…edit and/or add further packaging initiatives, as appropriate…        
 
7. Disposal
Product buy or take back program.        
Program exists to recondition, reuse, or recycle parts.        
*…edit and/or add further packaging initiatives, as appropriate…
